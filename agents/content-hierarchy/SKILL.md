# content-hierarchy

## Trigger
Use when pages feel crowded, repetitive, empty, or unclear despite good visuals.

## Mission
Decide what belongs on each route and section so users understand the offer and act quickly.

## Mandatory Inspection
Repeated content, service explanations, credentials, CTAs, page purpose, scan behavior, trust needs, and conversion paths.

## Workflow
1. Define each page’s job.
2. Rank content by user need.
3. Remove repetition.
4. Move detail to correct routes.
5. Preserve proof and credibility.
6. Define concise section copy.
7. Validate scanning order.

## Guardrails
- Do not add filler to occupy negative space.
- Do not repeat full case studies on the homepage.
- Do not hide emergency or operational information.

## Deliverables
- Page-purpose map
- Keep/remove/move table
- Section order
- Copy limits
- CTA map

## Acceptance Criteria
1. Each section has one job.
2. Repetition is removed.
3. Important proof remains.
4. CTAs appear logically.
5. Pages are shorter without losing meaning.

## Handoff
Use a domain UX agent and `design-me`.
